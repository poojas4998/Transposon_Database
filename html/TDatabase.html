Comparative Genomics of Select Vertebrate Transposons Consensus Sequences

About the Database Project

This is a database for a comparative genomics project on vertebrate transposons consensus sequences by a team of four members led by Professor Nelson Lau. The aim of this project is to study the transposon compositions in the genomes of four different species and to use SQL tools to characterize the transposon compositions and see how many transposons might be shared in sequence identity and length and type between the four different species. We have developed a user interface to allow people to interact with the data in the database through queries, and a series of help pages are developed for the database.

The 4 Select Vertebrate Species

Takifugu rubripes

Takifugu rubripes, commonly known as the Japanese pufferfish, is a marine fish species that is highly regarded for its unique ability to inflate itself like a balloon to deter predators. It is also a valuable model organism in biological research due to its small genome size and genome sequencing.

Heterocephalus glaber

Heterocephalus glaber, also known as the naked mole-rat, is a small, hairless mammal that is native to parts of East Africa. It has a unique social structure and is capable of surviving in low oxygen conditions, making it an important research model for studying aging, cancer, and metabolism.

Homo sapiens (T2T)

Homo sapiens, or humans, are a species of bipedal primates that have evolved to become the dominant species on the planet. With our advanced intelligence and complex social systems, humans have been able to develop advanced technology and make significant impacts on the environment.

Betta splendens

Betta splendens, also known as the Siamese fighting fish, is a popular freshwater fish species that is often kept as a pet. The males of the species are known for their aggressive behavior towards other males, and are often used in fish fights in certain parts of the world.
